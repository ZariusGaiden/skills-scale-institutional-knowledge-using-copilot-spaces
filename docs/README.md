## OctoAcme Project Management Processes — Summary

OctoAcme’s project management employs a five-phase lifecycle: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Close & Retrospective. Projects begin with a validated business need and stakeholder alignment via a Project One-pager, followed by backlog creation, estimation, risk identification, and clear Definitions of Done during planning. The execution phase leverages GitHub Projects for tracking (Backlog → Done), uses small, reviewable pull requests, and integrates automated testing and peer reviews into each workflow. Releases follow standardized checklists, including CI passing, security scanning, staging verification, and rollback readiness, before deploying to production. Retrospectives capture learnings and turn them into backlog action items.

Roles are clearly defined: Project Managers orchestrate delivery and risk management, Product Managers steer priorities and success metrics, Developers implement and test features, and QA/Testing teams validate acceptance criteria. Communication operates on a reliable cadence with daily standups, weekly syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates, plus risk escalation pathways through team, management, and sponsor levels as needed. Risks are managed through a living register reviewed and updated throughout the project. Quality assurance is ensured through layers of automation, manual checks, and pre-release requirements, making the OctoAcme approach adaptable, transparent, and focused on continuous improvement.
